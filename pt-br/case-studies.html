1) LATAM Delivery Playbook (2012–2023)

Empresa: Accuray (LATAM)

Produtos: CyberKnife • Radixact • TomoTherapy • TomoReNew

Escopo: Participated in all installs across a multi-country LATAM footprint (2012–2023).

Implementation Leadership Regulated Healthcare Partner/Distributor Management Go-Live Readiness Forecast Predictability

Contexto

Delivery required consistent execution across distributors, regulatory requirements, construction readiness variability, and cross-border logistics. Success meant predictable milestones from pre-sales through clinical go-live and a smooth transition into warranty/service operations.

Meu papel

Owned end-to-end execution from pre-sales → installation → go-live, and ensured readiness for warranty and ongoing service. Led alignment across Accuray internal teams, distributor organizations, and customer stakeholders (executive, technical, clinical).

O que fiz

  • Standardized execution and handoffs across the project lifecycle to improve quality and reduce rework.
  • Used Salesforce for major milestones and Smartsheet for detailed execution from Pre-Install to Go-Live.
  • Controlled readiness and quality via governed PDF checklists archived at close-out.
  • Coordinated across Accuray (Installation, Physics, Engineering, Sales, Ops, QA), distributors (FSE, QA, Service, Commercial, Leadership), and customer executives/technical/clinical teams.

Resultados

  • More efficient installs and reduced parts replacement through improved overall project quality.
  • Forecast variance improved from 12–24 months down to ≤6 months (remaining variance mainly due to construction permits).

Product: Radixact

Escalation Leadership Reliability Outcomes Cross-Functional Alignment Customer Trust

3) Uptime Model: Parts + Logistics Enablement

Empresa: Accuray (LATAM)

Foco: Reducing downtime risk by improving parts availability and logistics responsiveness

Operational Excellence Time-to-Resolution Regional Enablement Customer Outcomes

Contexto

In LATAM, uptime is often constrained by parts lead times and cross-border shipping. Improving customer outcomes required strengthening the operating model behind service responsiveness.

O que fiz

  • Deployed spare parts kits staged in each distributor country to reduce “waiting on parts” downtime exposure.
  • Enabled a regional logistics depot model in Mexico & Brazil to streamline fulfillment.
  • Coordinated specialized US-based freight forwarding optimized for LATAM shipments.

Resultados

  • Improved uptime by reducing delays tied to parts availability and shipping bottlenecks.

4) Sistema de Handover de Conectividade Unity (2026) — Habilitando treinamentos e go‑lives de Clinical Ops

Contexto

Clinical Operations required site-specific system configuration, hospital IT and network details to complete project milestones, plan onsite training, and execute successful go-lives. Data lived across multiple owners and channels, creating repeated follow-ups and delays.

Meu Papel

Designed and implemented the global handover approach as an individual contributor, aligning Clinical Ops needs with Systems Specialists inputs and escalation workflows.

Problema

  • Dados de conectividade do site estavam fragmentados entre pessoas, threads e documentos.
  • Clinical Ops precisava acionar Systems Specialists repetidamente para os mesmos inputs do site.
  • Conclusão de marcos e readiness de treinamento eram bloqueadas por dados faltantes/pouco claros.

Solução

  • Criei um dataset global no Teams para centralizar todo o conjunto de dados de conectividade de Clinical Ops por site.
  • Padronizei campos e um checklist de handover para que os inputs fossem completos e consistentes.
  • Defini ownership e timing para que os dados estivessem disponíveis antes de marcos, treinamento e go‑live.

O que padronizei (exemplos)

  • Project reference, site identity, hospital IT contact, Systems Specialist owner
  • Linac identifiers and naming conventions (e.g., MOSAIQ linac name)
  • MOSAIQ/Monaco versions, environment notes, non-standard configuration flags
  • Vendor account availability + access details, domain account requirements guidance
  • Clinical admin URL, SIP/checklist links

Impacto

  • Reduzi contatos repetidos com Systems Specialists e eliminei a “caça à informação” entre canais.
  • Melhorei a previsibilidade de marcos do projeto, planejamento de treinamento clínico onsite e readiness de go‑live.
  • Adotado em 10+ sites desde jan 2026 (e expandindo conforme novos sites entram em trial).

5) Playbook de Conectividade Unity (Piloto, 2026)

Papel: MR Linac Systems Specialist (Oct 2023–Present)

Ferramenta: Smartsheet template

Status: Deployed ~1 month ago; pilot rollout with Systems Specialists + PMs

Process Standardization Delivery Predictability Risk Management PM Enablement

Contexto

Unity connectivity spans interdependent steps and prerequisites across hospital IT readiness and software requirements. Execution quality depends on making ownership, dependencies, and readiness criteria visible early.

Problem

Connectivity projects can drift when tracking is inconsistent across sites, dependencies aren’t explicit, and prerequisites are discovered late—creating rework and coordination overhead.

O que fiz

Designed and built a standardized Smartsheet Unity Connectivity template as a single execution plan and “source of truth” for Systems Specialists and PMs, including phase-based workflow, ownership, status, % complete, target vs actual dates, dependency tracking, and risk-of-delay flagging.

Abordagem do piloto

Starting with Systems Specialists and PMs first to validate repeatability, adoption, and operational fit before expanding visibility to broader groups.

Resultados esperados (critérios de sucesso do piloto)

  • Menos pré‑requisitos perdidos e menos retrabalho de última hora
  • Execução mais previsível de marcos de conectividade
  • Menor overhead de coordenação por um plano compartilhado e vivo
