1) LATAM Delivery Playbook (2012–2023)

Company: Accuray (LATAM)

Products: CyberKnife • Radixact • TomoTherapy • TomoReNew (refurbished)

Scope: Participated in all installs across a multi-country LATAM footprint (2012–2023).

Implementation Leadership Regulated Healthcare Partner/Distributor Management Go-Live Readiness Forecast Predictability

Context

Delivery required consistent execution across distributors, regulatory requirements, construction readiness variability, and cross-border logistics. Success meant predictable milestones from pre-sales through clinical go-live and a smooth transition into warranty/service operations.

My role

Owned end-to-end execution from pre-sales → installation → go-live, and ensured readiness for warranty and ongoing service. Led alignment across Accuray internal teams, distributor organizations, and customer stakeholders (executive, technical, clinical).

What I did

  • Standardized execution and handoffs across the project lifecycle to improve quality and reduce rework.
  • Used Salesforce for major milestones and Smartsheet for detailed execution from Pre-Install to Go-Live.
  • Controlled readiness and quality via governed PDF checklists archived at close-out.
  • Coordinated across Accuray (Installation, Physics, Engineering, Sales, Ops, QA), distributors (FSE, QA, Service, Commercial, Leadership), and customer executives/technical/clinical teams.

Results

  • More efficient installs and reduced parts replacement through improved overall project quality.
  • Forecast variance improved from 12–24 months down to ≤6 months (remaining variance mainly due to construction permits).

2) Radixact La Paz Reliability Turnaround

Company: Accuray

Location: La Paz, Bolivia (high altitude)

Product: Radixact

Escalation Leadership Reliability Outcomes Cross-Functional Alignment Customer Trust

Context

A high-altitude deployment introduced non-standard technical requirements, creating risk to long-term reliability, uptime, and customer confidence.

My role

Led cross-functional coordination through a complex technical adaptation, balancing timeline impact with the long-term success criteria: stability, uptime, and confident clinical operations.

What I did

  • Aligned Installation, Physics, Engineering, QA, and Operations on the altitude-driven requirements gap and plan of record.
  • Drove development and deployment of a high-altitude hardware kit for the site.
  • Managed distributor execution and customer communications through go-live preparation.

Results

  • System became one of the most reliable in LATAM with rare downtime, largely manageable via preventive maintenance.

3) Uptime Model: Parts + Logistics Enablement

Company: Accuray (LATAM)

Focus: Reducing downtime risk by improving parts availability and logistics responsiveness

Operational Excellence Time-to-Resolution Regional Enablement Customer Outcomes

Context

In LATAM, uptime is often constrained by parts lead times and cross-border shipping. Improving customer outcomes required strengthening the operating model behind service responsiveness.

What I did

  • Deployed spare parts kits staged in each distributor country to reduce “waiting on parts” downtime exposure.
  • Enabled a regional logistics depot model in Mexico to streamline fulfillment.
  • Coordinated specialized US-based freight forwarding optimized for LATAM shipments.

Results

  • Improved uptime by reducing delays tied to parts availability and shipping bottlenecks.

4) Unity Clinical Ops Handover System (2026)

Role: MR Linac Systems Specialist (Oct 2023–Present)

Scope: Global Unity Systems team + Unity Clinical Operations

Rollout: 10+ sites onboarded since Jan 2026 (no retroactive backfill yet)

Operational Excellence Knowledge System Global Enablement Escalation Readiness

Problem

Connectivity and deployment-critical information was fragmented across email threads, ad-hoc messaging, meetings, and multiple internal systems. Some key details existed only with the Systems Specialist, creating delays and repeated internal coordination.

What I did

Created a standardized handover process from the Unity Systems team to Unity Clinical Operations and implemented a centralized connectivity dataset accessible via companywide Microsoft Teams (including mobile access).

What I standardized (examples)

  • Project reference, site identity, hospital IT contact, Systems Specialist owner
  • Linac identifiers and naming conventions (e.g., MOSAIQ linac name)
  • MOSAIQ/Monaco versions, environment notes, non-standard configuration flags
  • Vendor account availability + access details, domain account requirements guidance
  • Connectivity accounts, clinical admin URL, SIP/checklist links

Results

  • Adopted globally with 10+ sites added since Jan 2026.
  • Reduced friction with fewer meetings/calls and less “single person holds the data” dependency.
  • Saved time: typically a couple of hours previously spent searching across systems, plus additional hours of back-and-forth with specialists.
