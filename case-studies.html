Unity Connectivity Handover System — Enabling Clinical Ops Training & Go-Lives

Context

Clinical Operations required site-specific hospital IT and network details to complete project milestones, plan onsite training, and execute safe go-lives. Data lived across multiple owners and channels, creating repeated follow-ups and delays.

My role

Designed and implemented the global handover approach as an individual contributor, aligning Clinical Ops needs with Systems Specialists inputs and escalation workflows.

Problem

  • Site connectivity data was fragmented across people, threads, and documents.
  • Clinical Ops had to contact Systems Specialists repeatedly for the same site inputs.
  • Milestone completion and training readiness were blocked by missing/unclear data.

Solution

  • Built a Teams-based dataset to centralize the full Clinical Ops connectivity data set per site.
  • Standardized fields and a handover checklist so inputs were complete and consistent.
  • Defined ownership and timing so data was available ahead of milestones, training, and go-live.

What I standardized (examples)

  • Project reference, site identity, hospital IT contact, Systems Specialist owner
  • Linac identifiers and naming conventions (e.g., MOSAIQ linac name)
  • MOSAIQ/Monaco versions, environment notes, non-standard configuration flags
  • Vendor account availability + access details, domain account requirements guidance
  • Clinical admin URL, SIP/checklist links

Impact

  • Reduced repeat outreach to Systems Specialists and eliminated “hunt for info” across channels.
  • Improved predictability for project milestones, onsite clinical training planning, and go-live readiness.
  • Adopted across 10+ sites since Jan 2026 (and expanding as additional sites enter trial).
